The figure of merits --- # Standard pipeline --- ## Algo 1. Take a 1D descriptions of the events 2. Binning to extract summary statistics -> $n_i$ 3. Apply maximum likelihood point estimation -> $\hat \mu$ 4. Invert test statistic (profiled likelihood included) \begin{equation} \frac{H(\mu = \hat \mu)}{H(\mu \neq \hat \mu)} \end{equation} --- ## The model The model used to apply maximum likelihood is the binned poisson likelihood combined with the calibration. \begin{equation} L(\mu, \alpha) = L(Calib | \alpha) \times \prod_{i=0}^K Poisson(n_i | \mu \gamma_i + \beta_i) \end{equation} --- # Upgrades --- ## Upgrades chain 1. Best feature 2. Classifier 3. Robust classifier 4. Inference aware neural network 5. Upgrade model --- ## Best feature For all features use the standard pipeline on it. Choose the feature giving the smaller interval. --- ## Classifier Dimension reduction to 1D. Trying to separate signal events and background events. cf inverse fisher and Cramer Rao bound without systematics \begin{equation} \sum_{i=0}^K \frac{\gamma_i^2}{\mu \gamma_i + \beta_i } \leq \frac{(\sum_{i=0}^K \gamma_i)^2}{\sum_{i=0}^K \mu \gamma_i + \beta_i } \end{equation} --- ## Classifier Tries to concentrate all the information about $\mu$ inside one feature. --- ## Robust classifier Starting point for us. The classifier method does not include systematic effect. Build a 1D reduction robust to systematic effect. (Data augmentation, Pivot, Tangent Propagation) --- ## INFERNO Differentiable simulator (up to the parameters $\mu, \alpha$) allows to directly optimize the binning. --- ## Upgrade model The summary statistics $n_i$ contain mostly information about $\mu$ but both $\mu$ and $\alpha$ are required. Why not including more information extracted from the data ? --- ## Upgrade model Using a regressor (mamouth) trained to regress $\alpha$ we shoud extract as much information about $\alpha$ as possible. Then we include it in the model. \begin{equation} L(\mu, \alpha) = L_c(Calib | \alpha) \times L_e(extra | \alpha) \times \prod_{i=0}^K Poisson(n_i | \mu \gamma_i + \beta_i) \end{equation} --- # Experimental evidence --- ## On toys - robustness works - INFERNO works - extra info works --- ## On Higgs - difficult because signals << backgrounds - no method gives good results - Extra information still makes it better --- ## Need more bins for higgs --- # Figure of merits --- ## Mean squared error The empirical mean squared error of the maximum likelihood estimator. --- ## The length of a 1 $\sigma$ interval The average length of the estimated confidence interval. --- ## Coverage probability of a 1 $\sigma$ interval Empirical coverage probability of the interval. --- ## Conclusion --- # END
